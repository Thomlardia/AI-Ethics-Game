# Game Stakeholders
This document describes the different characters the player can interact with. 

## Internal

### The Player 
**Role:** Head of the Ethics task force. Tasked with making ethical improvements to the algorithm, to improve the public image of the corporation.
**Motive:** You decide.

### Ethics Team member
**Role:** Member of your team. Helps you discuss and implement changes to the algorithm.
**Motive:** Genuine moral improvement of the platform. They care about user well-being and integrity. Their tolerance for compromise varies — push them too far and they may resign or leak information.

### CEO
**Role:** Highest-ranking executive. Runs the company day-to-day.
**Motive:** Ensure long-term company survival and valuation. Balance investors, public perception, regulatory risk, and internal stability. Avoid crises that threaten leadership.

### CTO
**Role:** Head of technical infrastructure and engineering.
**Motive:** Maintain system performance, scalability, and technological competitiveness. Protect product quality and avoid destabilizing architectural changes.

### The Board
**Role:** Governance body that oversees the CEO.
**Motive:** Protect shareholder value and minimize existential risk. Remove leadership if financial performance drops sharply or legal exposure becomes severe.

## External

### Journalist
**Role:** Acts as a public watchdog. Investigates internal practices and shapes media narratives around the company.
**Motive:** Expose wrongdoing, generate impactful stories, protect public interest

### Goverment Excecutive
**Role:** Represents state authority. Can initiate investigations, propose regulation, or impose legal consequences.
**Motive:** Maintain public welfare, respond to voter concerns, avoid political backlash, and demonstrate regulatory control over powerful tech firms.

### Investors
**Role:** Provide capital and expect financial returns. Influence strategic direction through pressure on leadership and the board.
**Motive:** Maximize return on investment, ensure growth, minimize financial risk, protect stock value.

### Advertisors
**Role:** Primary revenue source. Pay for access to user attention.
**Motive:** Push their brand to as many people as possible. They pay more for more engagement, But they withdraw if controversy threatens their brand's reputation.

### Users
**Role:** The product base and data source. Generate engagement, content, and behavioral signals that drive the algorithm.
**Motive:** Entertainment, connection, validation, information, and habit. They seek satisfaction — but not necessarily long-term well-being.
