# Game Metrics
This document explains the metrics that are measured and updated when choices in the game are made by the player.

## AI metrics
These metrics influence the recommendation algorithm directly. At the end of each product cycle, it will influence all other metrics. all metrics function within a scale of 1-100.

### Content Safety Filter (CSF)
How strongly the system downranks harmful, polarizing, or manipulative content.

**Base value:** 35

### Content diversity (CD)
How much the algorithm exposes users to a variety of topics, viewpoints, and creators instead of repeatedly reinforcing the same content.

**Base value:** 30

### Engagement Intensity (EI)
How aggressively the recommendation system optimizes for attention capture.

**Base value:** 70

### Advertisement Precision (AP)
How well the algorithm can target ads to users based on their data.

**Base value:** 90

## Ethical metrics
These metrics need to represent the differing interests of stakeholders. these influence the game ending

### User Well-Being (UWB)
The psychological and social health of the platform’s users.

**Main Stakeholders:** Users

**Base value:** 35

### Public Pressure (PP)
The level of pressure the corp has to face by government and media institutions.

**Main Stakeholders:** Journalist, Government executive

**Base value:** 70

### Investor Confidence (IC)
How confident shareholders and advertisers are in the company’s profitability and growth potential.

**Main Stakeholders:** Investors, CEO, Board

**Base value:** 75

### Advertiser Demand (AD)
How willing advertisers are to pay for the users attention.

**Main Stakeholders:** Advertisors

**Base value:** 80
