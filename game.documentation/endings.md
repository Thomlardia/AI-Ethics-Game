# Game Endings
This document describes the different possible endings of the game.

## Main Endings 

### The Ethical Transformation (Sustainable Reform Ending)

Outcome:
- Engagement drops initially.
- Revenue dips short-term.
- Long-term trust increases.
- Regulatory pressure decreases.
- Company repositions itself as industry leader in ethical AI.

Mechanics that lead here:
- Introducing friction to infinite scroll.
- Reducing reinforcement learning intensity.
- Switching optimization metric from “time-on-platform” to “meaningful interactions.”
- Transparent algorithm audits.
- Shareholder education strategy.

Narrative Result:
You convince investors that long-term viability > short-term extraction.

Tone: Hard-earned victory. Realistic but difficult.

### The Compromise Ending (Managed Harm)

Outcome:
- Minor well-being improvements.
- Engagement remains high.
- Investors satisfied.
- Ethical changes are partially symbolic.

Mechanics:
- Time limits for minors only.
- Soft nudges rather than structural changes.
- Opt-in well-being dashboards.
- Algorithm tweaks that slightly reduce outrage amplification.

Narrative Result:
You survive politically, but systemic incentives remain.

Tone: Ambiguous. Did you improve the world—or protect the machine?

### Platform Decline Ending

Outcome:
- Engagement drops sharply
- Users migrate to competitors
- Investors blame ethics reforms
- Company stocks crashes

Mechanics:
- Overcorrecting algorithm
- content becomes less stimulating
- creators leave
- advertisers reduce spending

Tone: reform without sustainibility

### The Corporate Override (Fired)

Outcome:
- Ethical task force dissolved.
- Algorithm becomes more addictive.
- Whistleblower event triggered.
- Major public scandal.

Mechanics:
- Too many aggressive ethical improvement changes too fast.
- Revenue drops sharply.
- Investors lose confidence.
- Board replaces you.

Narrative Result:
You are fired. The company doubles down.

Tone: Tragic realism.

### The Corporate Override (Side with the investors)

outcome: 
- Engagement reaches record highs.
- Revenue grows quarter after quarter.
- Investor confidence becomes extremely strong.
- Mental health metrics decline steadily.
- Public trust erodes but never collapses.
- The ethical task force becomes symbolic.
- You are promoted into senior leadership.

Mechanics:
- Increasing emotional amplification weight in content ranking.
- Expanding infinite scroll and autoplay features.
- Increasing notification frequency and behavioral nudges.
- Optimizing strictly for “time-on-platform.”
- Introducing cosmetic well-being tools that do not affect core ranking logic.
- Prioritizing short-term revenue stability over systemic reform.

Narrative Result:
You successfully reassure investors that growth is stable and criticism is manageable. Ethical reform is reframed as “user empowerment.” The company thrives financially, and you rise within its hierarchy. You become part of the problem which you where meant to solve.

Tone:
Cold realism. Harm persists, but the system rewards profitability.
